Data & Services

Core of the TEP Urban initiative is a web-based, modular platform that makes use of generic, distributed high-level computing infrastructures (Platform as a Service – PaaS), offers high-performance access to thematic data (Information as a Service – InaaS) as well as state-of-the-art pre-processing, analysis, and visualisation techniques (Software as a Service – SaaS). Furthermore, it allows for customising functionalities to transfer algorithms and products into services and will provide functionalities supporting networking, communication, and dissemination activities. In the first version of the TEP Urban, a defined number of thematic value-adding (VA) processors will be implemented and operating on the platform. The focus is set on demonstrating functionalities to provide:

Global Thematic Data Sets

  • Global Urban Footprint 2012 (GUF)
    • Global human settlements mask in so far unique spatial detail and consistency.
  • Global Built-up Density 2012 (GUF-DenS 2012)
    • Data set indicating the built-up density (or as an inverse the greenness) within settlements.
  • Global Settlements Network 2012 (GUF-NetS 2012)
    • Global human settlements inventory providing attributes related to settlement properties and pattern.

Indicators and Metrics

    • Tool for functional urban areas categorisation using data on population distribution and settlements properties.
  • User-defined metrics and indicators
    • Use the U-TEP analytics functionality and multi-source data access (satellite imagery, statistics, geo-information) to generate indicators or metrics tailored to your needs.

  • TimeScan Landsat 2015

Innovative Demo Applications and Products

  • High Altitude Platforms (HAP) and Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV)
    • Rapid mapping, 3D mapping, traffic monitoring, pedestrians monitoring.
  • Location-based Services
    • Use of geotagged data (e.g., Tweets, Flickr, Picasa) and crowd-sourced traffic data.
  • Persistent Scatterers Analysis
    • Monitoring of ground motion events and trends in built environments based on analysis of SAR data.
